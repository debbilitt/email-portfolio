Points of Pride

MBA Victory

A team of four NYU MBA students won the 8th Annual Deloitte MBA National Case Competition. It’s the first time that Stern—which beat out Sloan (MIT), Tuck (Dartmouth), and Fuqua (Duke)—has taken the top honors.

Research Gains

The latest National Science Foundation’s Higher Education Research and Development survey ranked NYU at 23, up from 55 just seven years ago. An important indicator of the growing strength of NYU’s research enterprise.

Cinematic Arts Awards

Fourteen NYU alumni from Tisch School of the Arts, the College of Arts and Science, and the School of Law were honored with Emmy Awards this month. In film, last year’s national Student Academy Award–winner Kevin Wilson Jr. (Tisch ’18) went on to earn an Oscar nomination for Best Live Action Short Film this year, attending his first Oscars ceremony two months shy of graduation.

Nobel Prize in Economics at NYU

Stern’s Paul Romer wins the 2018 Nobel Prize in Economics, bringing the number of Nobel-winning economists currently serving on NYU’s faculty to four.

Law School Ranking

NYU School of Law ranked No.1 by Princeton Review when it comes to career prospects.

Growth in University R&D Spending

Figure 1: Over the last six years, NYU has cracked the top quartile of the NSF's top universities for R&D expenditures, and our rate of growth is significantly higher than that of any other institution in the US.
